Stage 01 — Brief & Script 02

Getting the Brief Right Before You Touch the Tool

The most common reason AI-generated videos miss the mark is an incomplete brief — not the AI.

Client Brief Checklist — Collect Before Writing a Word
  • Platform — Where will this live? (TikTok, LinkedIn, YouTube, Website)Platform determines aspect ratio, length, pacing, and voiceover tone. Decide before scripting.
  • One goal — What is the single action this video should drive?Awareness, link click, sign-up, or call booking. One video, one goal. Not three.
  • Audience signal — Who specifically is watching, and what do they already believe?Not "marketing managers" — "marketing managers at UK SMEs who think AI video looks cheap."
  • The tension — What problem, fear, or aspiration does this video resolve?Every converting video addresses one specific tension. Generic = ignored.
  • Brand constraints — Colours, tone, what to avoid, any compliance restrictions?Financial, legal, healthcare clients often have words or visuals that cannot appear. Confirm upfront.
  • Proof point — One number, result, or fact the viewer should remember."50,000 users", "3x faster", "GDPR compliant" — one claim anchors everything else.
5 Opening Hooks by Content Type
Challenge
"If you're still [doing X the old way], you're leaving [money/time/results] on the table."
Best for: Agency and B2B content. Creates mild tension without being confrontational.
Statistic
"[Specific number] UK [businesses / marketers / freelancers] are doing X — and most of them are getting Y wrong."
Best for: LinkedIn and YouTube. Named geography + profession signals relevant content immediately.
Contrarian
"[Common advice in your field] is actually costing you [outcome]. Here's what to do instead."
Best for: Thought leadership and social ads. Takes a position — generates saves and shares.
Problem
"[Specific painful scenario your audience recognises]. There's a reason that keeps happening."
Best for: Product demos and lead gen ads. Works when the problem is specific enough to feel personal.
Curiosity
"Most people don't realise [counterintuitive truth about your product or market]."
Best for: Short-form social. The word "most" creates implicit contrast — the viewer wants to be in the minority who does know.

Stage 03 — QC & Client Approval 04

Quality Control Before It Leaves Your Desk

The majority of revision requests come from issues that could be caught in 10 minutes of structured review.

Pre-Send QC Checklist — Complete Before Every Delivery
  • The 5-second testWatch the first 5 seconds with no audio. If the visual doesn't communicate something interesting on its own, regenerate the opening scene.
  • Brand compliance checkLogo visible? Correct colours in overlays? No competitor brand assets accidentally generated? No unintended faces or recognisable people?
  • Voiceover review at 1.0x speedListen for: mispronunciations (brand names, technical terms), unnatural pausing, incorrect emphasis. Regenerate the affected segment only if needed.
  • Caption accuracyCheck auto-generated captions against the script. AI voice-to-text can misread proper nouns. Correct before delivery.
  • CTA visible on-screenThe final CTA text or URL must appear as both voiceover and on-screen text simultaneously. Verify both are present.
  • Platform format checkConfirm export aspect ratio matches delivery platform. A 16:9 YouTube video delivered to TikTok will underperform regardless of content quality.
Client Approval SOP — Running Faster Review Cycles
What to Send with the Draft

Always deliver the first draft with:

  • → A one-line context note: what the video is designed to do
  • → Three specific feedback prompts (not "what do you think?")
  • → Your deadline for revision requests
Three Feedback Prompts That Work
  • → "Does the opening hook reflect how your audience describes the problem?"
  • → "Is there anything here that could conflict with your compliance requirements?"
  • → "Is the CTA clear enough for a first-time viewer?"
On Revision Scope

Define "one revision round" in your contract as: script-level changes or visual style changes, but not both in the same round. Clients who request simultaneous script and visual changes are effectively requesting a new video. Scoping this clearly prevents the most common cause of scope creep in video production work.

Upsell Opportunity

The 1-to-5 repurposing package is a billable deliverable, not a free addition. If your base rate is £250 per video, a "multi-platform package" (all 5 formats + caption file + publishing schedule) is a straightforward upsell to £375–£450. Most clients will take it — the incremental cost to you is 15 minutes of export work.

Generate 3, keep 1. For any paid media brief, generate three versions of the same script with different visual styles. The AI produces distinct outputs each time — no overlapping stock. A/B test all three in the first 48 hours, cut the lowest CTR, run the remaining two for a further 72 hours. This approach consistently outperforms single-version launches.
