What makes these templates work

  • Each template opens with a pattern interrupt — the first 3 seconds determine whether the viewer keeps watching
  • The problem is stated before the solution — audiences engage more when they feel understood, not sold to
  • All CTAs are low-friction — "start free" or "see how it works" outperforms "buy now" for professional audiences
  • Copy is written in British English — "video editor" not "videographer", "£" not "$", "agency" not "shop"
  • Every template is platform-agnostic — works on LinkedIn, YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, and TikTok

1
30-Second Social Ad
TikTok · Reels · YouTube Shorts 25–35 seconds Best for: Promoting a product or service
Script Framework — Fill in the brackets
Hook (0–3s)
"Are you still [doing painful thing the old way]?"
Use a behaviour your audience recognises immediately. Make it slightly uncomfortable.
Problem (3–10s)
"Most [target audience] spend [X hours/£X] every [week/month] on [pain point] — and the results still look generic."
Name the cost: time or money. UK professionals respond to both.
Solution (10–22s)
"[Product name] lets you [key benefit] in [timeframe]. [One differentiating feature]. [Second differentiating feature]."
Three beats max. Lead with the outcome, not the feature.
Proof (22–28s)
"[Number]+ [customers/businesses/agencies] are already using it to [result]."
Use a real number. Vague claims lose UK audiences fast.
CTA (28–33s)
"[Link in bio / Visit [url]] to start free — no credit card needed."
Low-friction CTA. Remove every possible barrier.
Example — Filled In (Marketing Agency)
Hook"Are you still spending your Sunday evenings editing client videos in Premiere Pro?"
Problem"Most UK agencies spend 8–12 hours per video, and clients still complain it looks stock."
Solution"Zebracat generates a fully unique, client-ready video from your script in under 2 minutes. No stock footage. British voiceovers. Brand kit built in."
Proof"50,000+ creators and agencies are already using it to scale their output."
CTA"Link in bio — start free today."

Template 2 of 5
2
Client Case Study Video
LinkedIn · YouTube · Website 60–90 seconds Best for: Agencies & freelancers showing results
Script Framework — Fill in the brackets
Client Intro (0–8s)
"[Client type/industry] came to us with a problem: [specific challenge they faced]."
Name the industry, not the client if confidential. Specific beats generic every time.
The Stakes (8–18s)
"They were [wasting X time/spending £X] with [old approach], and [negative outcome] was starting to hurt [metric]."
Make the consequence real. Money lost or time wasted are most relatable.
Our Approach (18–45s)
"We introduced [solution/tool/process]. [Step 1]. [Step 2]. [Step 3]. The whole workflow now takes [new timeframe]."
Three steps max. Brevity signals competence.
The Result (45–65s)
"[Client] went from [before metric] to [after metric] in [timeframe]. That's [X% improvement / £X saved]."
Numbers close deals. If you don't have exact figures, use ranges.
CTA (65–75s)
"If you're facing the same challenge, [visit / DM / book a call at url] — we'll show you how we'd apply the same approach to your business."
Example — Filled In (Video Production Agency)
Client"A UK e-commerce brand came to us needing 20 product videos per month — with a £500 production budget."
Stakes"Traditional filming would have cost them £3,000 per video. They were falling behind competitors on social, and ad performance was dropping."
Approach"We built a Zebracat-powered workflow: script in 10 minutes, AI video in 2, brand kit applied automatically. 20 videos delivered in 3 days."
Result"Cost per video dropped from £3,000 to £18. ROAS on their ad campaigns improved by 34% within 6 weeks."
CTA"Book a free 15-minute workflow audit at our website — link in bio."

3
Lead Generation Video Ad
LinkedIn Ads · Meta Ads · YouTube Pre-Roll 45–60 seconds Best for: Driving sign-ups, calls, or downloads
Script Framework — Fill in the brackets
Pattern Break (0–5s)
"[Surprising statistic or counterintuitive statement about your industry]."
Lead with data or a provocation. Avoid "Hi, I'm…" — you lose 70% of viewers in the first 3 seconds.
Qualify (5–15s)
"If you're a [specific role] at a [company type] in [location/sector], and you're dealing with [problem]..."
Narrow the audience. The right person should feel directly addressed.
Promise (15–30s)
"We've helped [X companies/clients] achieve [specific outcome] without [common objection or painful trade-off]."
Mechanism (30–48s)
"Here's how it works: [Step 1], [Step 2], [Step 3]. Most clients see [early result] within [timeframe]."
Name your process. A named process signals expertise and reduces perceived risk.
CTA (48–58s)
"Click below to [download / book / start] — it takes less than [2 minutes / 30 seconds]. [URL]"
Give a time estimate. It reduces drop-off on the landing page significantly.
Example — Filled In (SaaS / AI Tool)
Pattern Break"The average UK marketing team spends 14 hours a week creating video content. And most of it gets 200 views."
Qualify"If you're a marketing manager or agency owner spending real budget on video that isn't converting..."
Promise"We've helped 50,000+ creators produce scroll-stopping video content without hiring an editor or touching editing software."
Mechanism"Paste your script. Choose a style. Zebracat generates unique AI visuals and a British voiceover in under 2 minutes. Export in any format."
CTA"Click below to start free — no credit card, no editing skills required. Takes 90 seconds to set up."

Template 4 of 5
4
LinkedIn Thought Leadership Video
LinkedIn Feed · LinkedIn Newsletter 60–90 seconds Best for: Building authority, attracting inbound leads
Script Framework — Fill in the brackets
Controversy (0–6s)
"[Common belief in your industry] is wrong. Here's what's actually happening."
Take a position. Neutral content gets ignored on LinkedIn. Safe opinions generate no engagement.
The Reality (6–25s)
"Most [professionals in your field] are still [doing outdated approach] because [reason]. But in the past [12 months / 2 years], [shift that changed everything]."
The Insight (25–55s)
"What the best [job title / company type] are doing instead: [Point 1]. [Point 2]. [Point 3]. The result? [Measurable or observable outcome]."
Three points is the LinkedIn sweet spot. Each should be one sentence.
Takeaway (55–75s)
"The single most important thing you can do this week: [specific, actionable step]."
Make it implementable in under an hour. That's what gets saved and shared.
Soft CTA (75–85s)
"I post [topic] content every [frequency]. Follow me for more — or drop a comment if you're seeing the same thing."
Ask for engagement, not a sale. The conversion happens in the DMs.
Example — Filled In (Content Strategist / Agency Owner)
Controversy"The 'post consistently' advice is killing UK freelancers. Here's what's actually working."
Reality"Most freelancers are grinding out 5 posts a week with no strategy, burning out, and wondering why their pipeline is dry."
Insight"The highest-earning freelancers I know post 2x per week. But every post has a hook that targets a specific pain point, a story that proves expertise, and a low-friction CTA. Volume means nothing without relevance."
Takeaway"This week: pick your three best client results, turn each one into a 60-second video script, and generate all three in Zebracat in under 30 minutes."
Soft CTA"Follow me for weekly content strategy breakdowns — or comment 'SCRIPTS' and I'll send you the framework I use."

Zebracat tip: For LinkedIn, choose the 1:1 square format or 4:5 portrait — they take up more screen space in the feed. Use a professional avatar voice with a British accent. Generate a custom thumbnail with your key stat overlaid — LinkedIn auto-plays but viewers click for context.
